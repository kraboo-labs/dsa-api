Recitals

(1) Whereas Article 22(5) of the Digital Services Act requires the European Commission to publish the register of designated Trusted Flaggers “in an easily accessible and machine-readable format”;

(2) Whereas the register is in practice published as a server-rendered HTML page — no API, no JSON or CSV download, no change feed, no stable identifiers;

(3) Whereas platform compliance teams are nonetheless expected to verify flagger status, route Article 22 notices with priority, and prove who was designated when;

Now, therefore: dsa-api mirrors the official register every 6 hours, normalises it into a stable schema, tracks every addition, modification and removal, and serves it the way the regulation intended.

Article 2

Endpoints

GET /v1/trusted-flaggers

List & filter — by country, area of expertise, DSC, designation date, free-text search.

 GET /v1/trusted-flaggers/lookup

Resolve an email, domain or website to a flagger. “Is this notice actually from a Trusted Flagger?”

 GET /v1/trusted-flaggers/{id}/history

Every recorded change for one flagger — designations, edits, removals, restorations.

 GET /v1/changes

Register-wide changelog. Poll it, diff it, audit it.

 GET /v1/stats

Aggregates by country, area and DSC — the numbers at the top of this page come from here.

Full OpenAPI schema & interactive docs at docs.dsa-api.com.

Article 3
